Due to the large number of fighters and the infrequency of bouts many fighters will never face each other. This makes direct pairwise comparisons between fighters difficult. Bayesian inference overcomes this problem by incorporating expert opinions through prior distributions.
As the amount of data on a fighter increases, the fighter's ability will rely less on the prior and more on the actual data.
In addition, Bayesian inference can quantify uncertainty in the predictions of the fighters abilitities. Instead of a single point estimate, a distribution of plausible values is obtained. This offers an informative and nuanced understanding of the fighter's abilitites when data is limited.
